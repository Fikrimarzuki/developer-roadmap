Grouping Text in HTML

HTML has two main grouping elements: <div> (block-level) and <span> (inline).

1. <div> – Block-level Container

This is inside a <div>.
A div always starts on a new line and takes full width.

You can use div to group multiple elements together.

Example usage of <div>:

  • Layout sections
  • Cards or boxes
  • Containers for forms or components

2. <span> – Inline Container

The <span> element is used to highlight or style small pieces of text inside a sentence without breaking the line.

Notice how the span does not create a new line.

Example usage of <span>:

  • Highlight words (like a highlighter)
  • Change color of a specific word
  • Wrap short text for styling

Summary

<div> = block-level, creates structure, groups large sections.

<span> = inline-level, groups small pieces of text for styling.
